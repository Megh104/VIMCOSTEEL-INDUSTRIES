Category Details
Stainless Steel Grades 304, 316, 316L, 410, 430, Duplex 2205
304: Most common, excellent corrosion resistance.
316: Marine-grade, resists chloride corrosion.
316L: Low carbon version of 316 for welding.
410: High strength and wear resistance.
430: Ferritic grade, great for decorative uses.
Duplex 2205: High strength and exceptional corrosion resistance for harsh environments.
Specifications ASTM A240, ASTM A276, ASTM A479, ASTM A312
ASTM A240: Covers chromium and chromium-nickel stainless steel plates, sheets, and strips.
ASTM A276: For bars and rods, both hot-finished and cold-finished.
ASTM A479: Specifications for stainless steel wire and shapes.
ASTM A312: Standard for seamless and welded pipes for high-temperature applications.
Standards ASTM, BS, JIS, EN, DIN, AISI
ASTM: American standards for material quality.
BS: British standards ensuring precise tolerances.
JIS: Japanese standards for industry precision.
EN: European norms for manufacturing quality.
DIN: German engineering standards.
AISI: American Iron and Steel Institute codes.
Finishing Options Mirror Finish, Matte, Brushed, Polished, No.4 Finish, Pickled Finish, Sandblasted, and more.
Mirror Finish: High gloss for aesthetic use.
Matte: Smooth, non-reflective surface.
Brushed: Fine texture with parallel lines.
No.4 Finish: General-purpose polished finish.
Pickled Finish: Acid-treated to remove impurities.
Sandblasted: Durable matte texture for industrial use.
